**Definition**: 
___
It is the implantation of fertilized ovum outside the normal uterine cavity.

**Incidence**: 
___
- 2-3% of all pregnancies 
- ampular 85%
- isthmus 8%
- cornual < 2%
- ovary  < 2%
- cervical  < 2%
- Abdominal  < 2%

**Diagnostic criteria:** 
___
- **Spiegelberg' criteria (ovarian)**
    -  Fallopian tube must be intact.
    - Ovarian fossa must contain the gestation.
    - Ovarian ligament must connect the gestation to the uterus
    -  Histological the gestational sac must contain ovarian tissue
- **Studdiford criteria  (Abdominal )**
    1. Fallopian tubes and ovaries are intact
	2. Uterus is intact i.e no utero peritoneal fistula
	3. Peritoneal surface and gestation are related 
- **Rubin's criteria(Cervical)**
    - Cervical gland opposite the placental attachment 
    - Placenta intimately attached to the cervix
    - Placenta implanted below the level of entry of the uterine artery and the uterovesical peritoneal reflection
    - No fetal elements in the uterine cavity.
- **Paalman and McEllin(cervical clinical )**
     - painless **PV - bleeding** 
     - Hourglass **cervix/uterus**
     - **placenta** intimately attach to the cervix 
     - snug **internal os**
**Causes**
___
**Congenital**: 
 - Tubal tortuosity 
 - partial stenosis
 - Accessory Ostia
 - congenital diverticula
**Acquired** 
  - **Inflammatory**: PID, septic abortion, purperal sepsis 
  - **Surgical**: Tubal reconstruction, tubal recanalization, BTL
  - **Miscellaneous**: IUCD, POP,  Endometriosis, ART, previous ectopic
