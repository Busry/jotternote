**Definition**: The presence of endometrial glands and stroma outside of the normal location.

**Epidemiology:** It affects:- 
- 10% of women of reproductive age, 
- 20 -50% infertility patients.
- 40 -60% of patients with chronic pelvic pain.

**Pathophysiology:** 
- Retrograde menstruation theory(Samson's)
- Stem cell theory (Valentijn)
- Coelomic metaplasia (Mayer)
- Vascular and lymphatic metastasis theory (Moore)
- Mullerian remnant theory
- Implantation theory
- Genetic immunological factors

**Risk factors**
-  Nulliparous
- Early menarche 
- Uterine or cervical malformations 
- Low BMI
-  High eostrogen exposure 
- Family history

**Protective factor
-  Multiparity
- Current use of oral contraceptives 
- High BMI
- Regular exercise
-  Fish and omega3 fatty acids

**Clinical Features**
- Chronic pelvic pain
- Dysmenorrhoea
- Dyspareunia

- Subfertility
- Bowel: Dyschezia, Hematochezia
- bladder symptoms: Dysuria, hematuria
- Diaphragm: Right hypochondrial and right shoulder pain- cyclical
- Lung: Catamenial Haemoptysis- coughing up blood during menstrual cycle
- Sciatalgia

**Examination**
___ 
- Pain trigger point
- Tender nodular masses along uterosacral ligament
	- Adnexa mass
	- pelvic tenderness
	- fixed uterine retroversion
	- painful uterine mobilization
	- Visible lesions in the vagina

**Investigation**
- Transvaginal ultrasound
- MRI for adenomyosis and deep infiltrating endometriosis
- Laparoscopy (gold standard for endometriosis)

**Image features**
- Endometrioma
- Nodules that are deep infiltrating

**Classification**
- **Severity** - Revised American Society for Reproductive Medicine (rASRM) 
	- Stage I ( minimal: 1- 5 points): implants - few and superficial.  Adhesions - non.
	- Stage II ( mild: 6 - 15 points): implants - more and deeper.  Adhesions - minimal.
	- Stage III ( moderate 16 - 40 points): implants - deep and endometriomas - small.  Adhesions - moderate.
	- Stage IV (severe > 40 points): Endometriomas - large.  Adhesions - dense. Organs - stuck together.
	
- **Location and depth (Enzian Classification)**
	- Compartment: 
		- A - Rectovaginal septum and vagina, 
		- B - Uterosacral ligament and pelvic side wall, 
		- C - Rectum and sigmoid colon,
		- F - Extra genital involvement (Bladder, ureters, bowel and
		- Diaphragm)
	- Grading:
		- 1: <1cm
		- 2: 1 - 3 cm
		- 3: >3cm
- **Endometriosis Fertility index**
	- Scores 0 -10 
	- Higher score =  better fertility prognosis
- **Symptom-Based/Clinical Classification**
	- superficial, 
	- Ovarian 
	- Deep (DIE)

**Treatment**
- Analgesia and NSAIDs
- Hormonal therapy (COCP, progestogens, GnRH analogues Visanne, Ryeqo, Mirena. Qlaira).
- Aromatase inhibitor
- Fertility preservation considerations
- Surgical excision for refractory cases- laparoscopic and Robotic.
	- Hysterectomy
	- Excision of endometrioma
	- Resection of endometriotic nodles or bowels

**Complication**
- Chronic pain and reduced quality of life
- Infertility
- Psychological impact
- Surgical risks
